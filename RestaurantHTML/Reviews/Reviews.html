The New York Times

Pete Wells

There are restaurants you go to, and then there’s Dorsia—where you ascend. Gilded in minimalist opulence, Dorsia curates an experience that flirts with perfection but, like its fabled reservations, remains just out of reach. The sea urchin ceviche with yuzu foam whispers to the soul, though the Wagyu with activated charcoal ash felt more performative than profound. Still, Dorsia remains a culinary unicorn—rare, elusive, and wholly intoxicating.

The Wall Street Journal

Sophia Teller

Dorsia doesn’t just serve dinner—it serves a statement. The precision of execution, from amuse-bouche to digestif, borders on the algorithmic. Truffle-infused monkfish, paired with a 30-year-old Riesling, delivers both decadence and restraint. For the Wall Street elite, it’s not just about taste, it’s about territory. And Dorsia’s still king.

New York Post

Richie Flair

Heard of Dorsia? Of course you have. It’s the club you can’t get into, unless you’re rich, famous, or both. But here’s the kicker—it’s actually worth it. Lobster risotto that melts in your mouth and a dessert cart that belongs in the Met. Bring your black card and leave your modesty at home.

Critic Reviews

Thorne Blake

Fork & Fury

This restaurant gave me an existential crisis... in a good way. Dorsia is like being punched in the palate by an angel. Their black garlic panna cotta served on a literal mirror made me weep. The maître d' said nothing the entire night, but I felt seen. It's food as fashion, service as silent judgment, and I live for it.

Carmela Rouge

Spice & Vice Quarterly

Orgasmic. Outlandish. Overbooked. From the velvet-gloved doorman to the dessert sommelier (yes, that’s real), Dorsia is designed for those who eat with their eyes and pay with their egos. The foie gras popsicle? Genius. The absinthe crème brûlée? Sinful. Just don't ask for substitutions—you’ll be politely escorted into existential despair.

Everette Moss

The Hipster Digest

Too mainstream to be cool, too delicious to ignore. Dorsia is the paradox of our time: a temple of exclusivity serving genuinely innovative, farm-to-fork cuisine. Sure, the reservation system feels like a psychological experiment, but that duck confit ravioli made me forget my ex. Temporarily.

Lana Velvet

Decadent Monthly

Dorsia isn’t just dinner—it’s a decadent dream sequence. The champagne was older than my therapist and twice as effective. I watched a couple break up and reunite between the amuse-bouche and the bone marrow crème brûlée. The flavors? Bold. The mood? Cinematic. The bill? Unspeakable. Worth every dramatic penny.
